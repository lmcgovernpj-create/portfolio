How I Work

01

Diagnose

Assess before prescribing. I start with needs and root-cause analysis to find out whether it's actually a training problem, or something else wearing that costume.

02

Design

Analyze before designing. Targeted blended, eLearning, and performance-support solutions, mapped to the real gap and the people who have to live with it.

03

Implement

Meet people where they are. Phased rollouts, plain-language job aids, and adoption support that holds up across multiple sites and past launch day.

04

Measure

Measure before declaring success. Outcomes checked against business goals, with an emphasis on whether behavior actually changed on the job.

Most people jump to solutions. I was trained to resist that instinct: assess, analyze, then build. It's the foundation under everything else here.

About Lori

I'm an instructional designer and performance improvement specialist who'd rather find the real problem than ship a fast answer to the wrong one.

My path ran through nine years in channel sales, classroom teaching, and more than a decade leading district-wide learning across eight campuses. That mix is the point. I've carried a quota, stood in front of a room, and rebuilt the systems behind both, so I read a performance gap from the business side and the human side at once.

Lately I work at the intersection of learning science, human performance, and AI in L&D, with a bias toward structured design over guesswork. I'm completing an M.Ed in Instructional Design & Performance Technology and a Human Performance Improvement certificate, and I'm comfortable working at both levels: a targeted training solution, or a fuller look at what's driving the gap.

  • Technology adoption supportFacilitating the adoption of new learning and technology tools so they actually get used.
  • Stakeholder collaborationWorking closely with leaders and SMEs to keep solutions aligned to organizational goals.
  • Outcome-oriented focusStrategies that lift engagement, improve retention, and deliver sustainable impact.
  • Effective training solutionsPrograms designed for rapid learning, comprehension, and on-the-job transfer.

About this site

Built with intent: AI-assisted and accessible by design

This portfolio was designed and built through AI-assisted workflows and structured prompt engineering, the same approach I use for content development and rapid prototyping. The point isn't novelty for its own sake. It's a demonstration: clear problem framing, deliberate iteration, and tools used with intent rather than guesswork.

It's also built to meet WCAG accessibility guidelines. The site is keyboard navigable, color-contrast checked, screen-reader friendly, and respectful of reduced-motion settings. Inclusive design isn't something I bolt on at the end. It's how I work.
